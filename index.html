Audio samples from "Robust neural codec language modeling with phoneme position prediction for zero-shot TTS"

Authors: Chunhui Lu, Xue Wen, Liming Song, Junkwang Oh

Abstract:
Though large language models (LLMs) based methods have achieved remarkable progress in zero-shot Text-to-Speech (TTS) synthesis, they suffer from robustness issues such as mispronunciation, word skipping, and word repeating. To address the robustness issue, we propose to incorporate the phoneme position prediction into the LLM-based TTS model. More concretely, given the input phoneme sequence as condition, our model predicts both the acoustic code and the position of its corresponding phoneme within the input sequence synchronously. The synchronous predicted targets are then fed back into the model as conditions for subsequent predictions, which guarantees the accuracy and completeness of alignment between acoustic codes and phonemes. Experimental results demonstrate that, compared to a strong baseline method, our system significantly reduces phoneme skipping and repeating alignment errors on challenging sentences, achieving a 68.8% relative reduction in character error rate, while performs comparably in zero-shot TTS evaluations.
 

This pages contains a set of speech samples used in our evaluations.All utterances and speakers are unseen in model training.

 

Compared systems

    GT: ground-truth audio reconstructed by the UniAudio's codec model.
    VALL-E: Implemented exactly following VALL-E, without incorporating any robustness improving techniques.
    VALL-E R: Following VALL-E R, the system predicts acoustic codes and their corresponding phoneme identities synchronously. For a fair comparison with our system, we use greedy decoding for phoneme prediction.
    OURS: Our proposed method.
